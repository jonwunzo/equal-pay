💰 The Revenue Reality

Some athletes and analysts believe that pay should reflect the revenue you generate. Sports, like any industry, reward performance and profit. UFC champion Ronda Rousey once said, “I’m the highest-paid fighter not because they wanted to do something nice for the ladies, but because I bring in the most money.”

  • Men’s World Cup revenue: $6 billion
  • Women’s World Cup revenue: $131 million

📜 Historical Inequality

The gender pay gap in sports didn’t happen overnight. For years, women were excluded from playing, underfunded when allowed, and rarely broadcasted. It’s not just about how much women make now—it’s about the opportunities they were denied for decades.

  • The Equal Pay Act of 1963
  • Systemic underinvestment in women’s sports
  • Movements pushing for equity

🌍 What’s Changing?

There is real momentum. Netflix is partnering with FIFA to stream future Women’s World Cups. NWSL ditched its draft system to guarantee better pay. Sponsorships are growing. Change isn’t just demanded—it’s happening.

  • ✅ More media visibility
  • ✅ New pay structures
  • ✅ Investment in women’s leagues
